# Ethical Red Lines

Civitas is a trust tool, which means it can do real harm if it's careless. These
are the lines it will not cross. They are product requirements, not marketing.

1. **No "bad member" score.** Civitas never ranks, grades, or scores individual
   people. Signals describe the health of the *community*, not the worth of a person.
2. **No replacing human judgment.** Automation flags; humans decide. Consequential
   actions require a person.
3. **No black-box decisions.** Every consequential moderation action produces a
   Decision Receipt explaining what happened and why.
4. **No private data in public dashboards.** Insights aggregate; evidence is
   minimized and never exposes private user data.
5. **No "report volume = guilt."** Coordinated or mass reporting cannot trigger an
   action on its own. A rule must apply and a human must review.
6. **Signals are prompts, not truth.** A flag is a reason to *look*, never a verdict.
   The screening is transparent pattern-matching and is wrong sometimes by design.
7. **Always an explanation and an appeal.** No silent removals. Every decision is
   legible and contestable.

## Why this matters
A "community health" dashboard can quietly become a surveillance tool that targets
unpopular members. Civitas is built to make that misuse hard: per-person scoring
doesn't exist, rescue/insight data isn't a public hit-list, and every enforcement
step is explained and appealable.
