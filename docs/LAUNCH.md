## D. Twitter/X thread

1. Most online communities don't fail because they lack features. They fail when trust breaks. I built Civitas to explore a different model. 🧵
2. Trust breaks quietly: unclear rules, unexplained decisions, inconsistent mods, ignored newcomers, no appeals. By the time it shows, it's late.
3. So Civitas isn't "moderation software." It's a trust layer with five parts.
4. Community Charter: a warm, public page for what a community protects and how it decides. Shareable. It becomes a marketing asset for the community itself.
5. Post Coach: instead of punishing bad posts, it helps members write good ones — community-aware tips, live, before publishing.
6. Decision Receipts: every decision shows the rule, the reason, who reviewed it, and how to appeal. People accept outcomes when they understand the process.
7. Trust Dashboard: founders see whether the community is getting healthier or more fragile — and what to do this week.
8. Member-powered Review: trusted members help with borderline cases in a calm, limited workspace. Stewards, not police.
9. It's an experiment. Screening is pattern-based (not real ML yet), the deploy is a demo. I'd rather be honest than oversell.
10. Live demo: [DEMO_URL] · Code: [GITHUB_URL]. Feedback very welcome.
